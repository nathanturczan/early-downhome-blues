# Glossary

Source: Jeff Titon, *Early Downhome Blues*

## Pitch and Scale

- **Keynote**: The tonal center (C in this system). C and C' are the most frequent tonal rest points.

- **Scale**: Simply identifies the pitches in a group of songs.

- **Mode**: Indicates the potential uses of the pitches.

## Complexes

- **Complex**: A group of pitches in close proximity which function together. The microtonal variants within a complex (flat, flat slightly sharp, natural slightly flat, natural) are used for contrast and emphasis.

- **E Complex**: Eb, Eb slightly sharp, E slightly flat, E. Total stem count: 346.

- **G Complex**: Gb, G slightly flat, G. Total stem count: 471.

- **B Complex**: Bb, Bb slightly sharp, B. Total stem count: 108.

- **E' Complex**: Eb', Eb' slightly sharp, E' slightly flat, E'. Total stem count: 204.

## Melodic Motion

- **Pendular motion**: Back-and-forth movement between two pitches (e.g., Eb operating as a pendulum point with C).

- **Passing tone**: A pitch used to connect two other pitches (e.g., A as a passing tone between G and Bb complex).

- **Arpeggio**: Upward movement through chord tones (e.g., from C to E to G).

## Song Structure

- **S**: Downhome blues song.

- **Z1, Z2, Z3...**: Stanzas within a song.

- **1, 2, 3**: Lines within a stanza (4 measures each).

- **a, b, c, d, e, f**: Phrases within lines (2 per line).
